The

Morris Worm

1988

the world’s first major cycber attack!

what

happened?

The 2nd of November 1988, marked a turning point in the history of computer security when Robert Morris unleashed a program known as a worm on to the internet from a computer at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. In a matter of time, this program propagated itself onto 1000’s of computers running on UNIX operating systems (Marsan, 2008).

how

did it happen?

The Morris worm took advantage of vulnerabilities in three UNIX programs:
sendmail, fingerd and rexec.
The program consisted of a main program and a vector program that was designed with the aim of gaining a foothold in as many machines while going unnoticed (Orman, 2003). However, the flaws contained within the program made this impossible.
Although the code was supposed to self-destruct on infection and prevent reinfection, an error in the code caused this step to be skipped 1 in 7 times, as a result it continued to create multiple copies that resulted in too many incoming requests that used up too many system resources. As a result this led to 10% of the computers connected to the network being forcibly shutdown (Spafford, 2018).

what was the

cost?

Back in 1988, the internet was drastically different from the internet we know now. Hence, it was mostly used by government organizations and universities to share information. There was also very little concern regarding security on the internet and in the UNIX operating systems.

Consequently, 6000 computers connected were infected by the worm resulting in damages worth $100,000 (The Morris Worm, 2018).

what were the

lessons learnt?

  1. Exposed the vulnerabilities of the internet and in 3 major programs in the UNIX operating system (Orman, 2003).
  2. Exposed the lack of knowledge of system administrators in configuring the sendmail program which exposed the system to unauthorized access.
  3. Led to the creation of the Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT) which consists of a community of experts on computer security who develop solutions for security flaws, provide information and training on network, computer, and internet security (CERT, n.d.; Orman, 2003; What is a CERT, anyway?, 2020).
  4. Creation of firewalls that were designed to prevent unauthorized access to computers and the networks they are connected to (Orman, 2003).
